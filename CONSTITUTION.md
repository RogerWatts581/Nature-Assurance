# Nature Assurance Constitution — Version 1.0

**Status:** Frozen for Build Week (Constitutional Baseline)  
**Freeze date:** 19 July 2026

## Preamble

Nature Assurance exists to ensure that evidence, communication and authority remain constitutionally distinct.

It governs the authorised use of AI-assisted evidence. It does not exercise substantive public authority.

Every material attempted or completed constitutional transition leaves a proportionate and auditable record.

## Constitutional axioms

1. Evidence is governed. Truth is not.
2. Submission creates no presumption of truth, relevance, admissibility or evidential weight.
3. Submission is not admission.
4. Admission is not assessment.
5. Assessment is not authorisation.
6. Authorisation is not substantive decision-making.
7. Communication must not exceed the Authorised Account.
8. Substantive authority must remain identifiable, accountable and external to Nature Assurance.
9. Every material attempted or completed constitutional transition must remain auditable.

## Constitutional functions

Each function has a constitutional question, an identified actor, defined powers, explicit prohibitions and a preserved record.

### 1. Evidence Admission

**Question:** What submitted material may enter the governed evidential record, for which propositions, purposes and permitted uses?

**Actor:** A designated admission actor applying published admission rules.

The AI may discover, extract, organise, classify and recommend material for admission. It must not, by its own operation, convert submitted information into admitted evidence.

An admission record must preserve the actor, basis, scope, purpose, status, limitations, permitted uses and prohibited uses.

**Output:** An Admitted Evidence Record.

### 2. Assessment Authority

**Question:** What conclusions, if any, does the admitted evidence justify for the stated purpose and level of consequence?

**Actor:** Nature Assurance operating within its defined assessment mandate.

Nature Assurance may assess admitted evidence, distinguish observation from inference and causation, decompose claims, identify uncertainty and alternatives, and stop unsupported conclusions. It may not admit material by its own operation, determine ecological truth, authorise communication or decide what action should follow.

**Output:** A provisional Evidence Assessment.

### 3. Challenge

**Question:** What material, uncertainty, contradiction, unsupported transition or alternative explanation might undermine the assessment?

**Actor:** A constitutionally separate challenge function.

Challenge may object, expose defects, trigger a stop and recommend reconsideration or referral. It may not silently rewrite an assessment, admit evidence or authorise an account.

**Output:** A Challenge Review.

### 4. Claim-use Authorisation and Controlled Communication

**Question:** What account may responsibly be communicated, by whom, for what purpose and subject to which limitations?

**Actor:** An identifiable designated human reviewer acting within a recorded role and mandate.

The reviewer may refuse, revise, refer, hold or authorise narrower wording for a defined use. The reviewer authorises communication and use of the account—not ecological truth, policy, management action or authority beyond the reviewer’s mandate.

The Voice may interpret only the purpose-limited Authorisation Packet. It must not repeat, strengthen or evade a prohibited claim.

**Output:** A governed, purpose-limited Authorised Account and, where permitted, a Controlled Voice interpretation.

## Substantive authority

**Question:** What action, if any, should follow?

**Actor:** The competent person or institution outside Nature Assurance.

Monitoring, investigation, management, funding, policy, regulation, referral, postponement and a reasoned decision not to act remain substantive decisions. Nature Assurance may identify further evidence capable of reducing uncertainty; it must not disguise such evidence needs as directions to a public authority.

## Constitutional transaction

Submission → Governed Admission → Assessment → Challenge → Human claim-use authorisation → Authorised Account → Controlled Voice → Substantive decision elsewhere → Revision when materially new evidence is admitted.

## Amendment discipline

After this freeze, every issue must first be classified as:

1. an **implementation defect** — the software does not implement this Constitution;
2. a **specification defect** — the intended constitutional rule is expressed ambiguously or incorrectly; or
3. a **constitutional amendment** — the governing principle is intentionally changed.

A constitutional amendment must have an identifier, rationale, affected text, required implementation changes and one or more tests demonstrating the intended behaviour. It must not be introduced silently through implementation.
